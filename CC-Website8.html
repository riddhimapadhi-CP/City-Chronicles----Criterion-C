About the Project

The City Chronicles podcast was created to amplify civic voices in Kharadi. We highlighted issues like waste management, water supply, air pollution, road maintenance, and drainage systems, making them relatable to everyday citizens. By blending resident interviews, expert insights, and fieldwork, we produced episodes that encouraged awareness, dialogue, and action. The project became a platform where local concerns were documented and connected to broader themes of sustainability and responsibility.

Process & Action (Criterion C)

Creating City Chronicles involved clear planning and creativity. We finalized the idea and success criteria, scripted episodes with a balance of research and storytelling, and coordinated recordings indoors and outdoors. Editing refined sound quality and transitions, while promotion through visuals and social media helped reach listeners. The process taught us lessons in planning, collaboration, and adaptability.

  • Finalized idea and success criteria
  • Scripted and recorded episodes
  • Edited audio for clarity and flow
  • Promoted podcast via social media

Reflection (Criterion D)

Strengths included the creative format, authentic voices, and teamwork. Weaknesses were limited reach and occasional technical glitches, showing the need for stronger promotion and more diverse interviews. Improvements could include partnerships, interactive elements, and experimenting with video formats.

The project extended my knowledge of service learning, showing how communication tools make civic issues accessible. I developed ATL skills in research, communication, collaboration, and self-management, and grew as a communicator, caring and reflective learner. Most importantly, the experience reinforced that civic awareness begins with listening, documenting, and sharing — and that even student-led initiatives can spark meaningful change.
